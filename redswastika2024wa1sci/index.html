Question 1

2 marks

The diagram below shows how some living things are grouped.

Which of the following about organisms K, L, M, and N, are correct?

  1. K is a reptile.
  2. L is a fish.
  3. M is a bird.
  4. N is a mammal.

Answer

Option 1. K lays eggs and has scales, so K is a reptile — correct. L lays eggs but has no scales, so L is an amphibian or insect (not necessarily a fish), making option (2) wrong. M does not lay eggs but has hair, so M is a mammal (not a bird), making option (3) wrong. N does not lay eggs and has no hair, so N is not a mammal, making option (4) wrong. Only option (1) is correct.

Question 5

2 marks

Ken fills a glass of water to the brim. After adding a few ice cubes into the glass of water, he observed that the water overflowed.

Which of the following best explain(s) Ken's observations?

A  Ice occupies space.
B  Water has no definite shape.
C  Water has no definite volume.
D  Water exists in three different states.

  1. A only
  2. A and B only
  3. A and C only
  4. B, C and D only

Answer

Option 2. The ice cubes are a solid that occupies space (A), so adding them pushes the water up and out of the full glass. Water has no definite shape (B), so it can flow over the brim and overflow. Statement C is wrong — water (a liquid) has a definite volume. Statement D is irrelevant to why the water overflows. So only A and B explain the observation — option (2).

Which of the following can be concluded about object X?

A  Object X is a magnet.
B  Object X can attract an iron nail.
C  Object X can be attracted to another magnet.

  1. A only
  2. C only
  3. A and B only
  4. A, B and C

Answer

Option 4. Only a magnet can be repelled by another magnet, so X must be a magnet (A). Because X is a magnet, it can attract a magnetic material such as an iron nail (B). A magnet has two poles, so the opposite pole of X can be attracted to another magnet (C). All three conclusions are correct — option (4).

Question 7

2 marks

Lynette conducted an experiment on springs X and Y. She hung different loads, one at a time on the springs and measured the lengths of the springs. Her results are shown in the graph below.

Which of the following statement(s) is / are correct?

A  Spring X is stiffer than spring Y.
B  Spring Y extends by 25 cm when a load of 8 g is hung on it.
C  Spring X is shorter than spring Y when a load of 14 g is hung on each of them.

  1. A only
  2. B only
  3. A and C only
  4. B and C only

Answer

Option 3. Spring X stretches less than spring Y for the same load (its line is less steep), so spring X is stiffer (A correct). Both springs start at 15 cm; at a load of 8 g, spring Y's length is about 30 cm, so it extends by 15 cm, not 25 cm (B wrong). At a load of 14 g, spring X is shorter than spring Y because X extends less (C correct). So A and C are correct — option (3).

Question 9

2 marks

Ethan poured an equal amount of hot water at 90 °C into three identical cups, E, F, G, made of different materials. He recorded the temperature of the water for 15 minutes in the graph below.

After 15 minutes, he poured the hot water out and waited for a few hours for the cups to return to room temperature. He then poured water at 5 °C into the three cups. He recorded the temperature of water after 30 minutes. The temperature of water in cup F was 15 °C after 30 minutes.

Which of the following correctly show the possible temperature of the water in cups E and G?

Cup ECup G
(1)2012
(2)2018
(3)1220
(4)1214

Answer

Option 3. In the cooling graph, cup E loses heat the slowest and cup G loses heat the fastest, so E is the best heat insulator and G is the poorest. A good insulator also lets heat enter slowly. With cold water (5 °C), the best insulator (E) gains heat the slowest and stays coldest, while the poorest insulator (G) gains heat the fastest and becomes warmest. Since cup F reached 15 °C, cup E must be cooler than 15 °C (12 °C) and cup G must be warmer than 15 °C (20 °C). This gives E = 12, G = 20 — option (3).

Question 10

4 marks

The diagram below shows an air-tight bottle connected to a pump. The volume of the bottle is 100 cm³. The volume of water and air in the bottle is 30 cm³ and 70 cm³, respectively at first.

(a) Using the pump, 10 cm³ of water and 20 cm³ of air are added into the bottle. What is the final volume of air in the bottle? Explain your answer. (2m)

Katie connected two empty bottles using a glass tube. A drop of ink is placed in the middle of the glass tube as shown below.

She has two tubs of water at different temperatures.

(b) Without disconnecting the apparatus above, suggest and explain how Katie can make use of the tubs of water to make the ink drop move towards bottle B. (2m)

Answer

(a) The final volume of air is 60 cm³. Adding 10 cm³ of water makes the volume of water 30 + 10 = 40 cm³. The bottle is air-tight and has a fixed volume of 100 cm³, so the air can be compressed and occupies the remaining space of 100 − 40 = 60 cm³.
(b) Katie should place bottle A into tub Y (the hot water at 90 °C) — or place bottle B into tub X (the cold water). The air in bottle A gains heat from the hot water, so the air expands and pushes the ink drop along the glass tube towards bottle B.

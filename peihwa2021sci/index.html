State the difference between evaporation and boiling.

(b)(i)

He observed some water droplets forming on the set-up after a few minutes. On the diagram below, draw the water droplets observed by Jason.

(b)(ii)

Explain how the water droplets formed.

Answer

(a)

Evaporation can occur at any temperature (at the surface of the liquid), while boiling only occurs at a fixed temperature (the boiling point, 100°C for water at standard pressure) throughout the liquid.

(b)(i)
(b)(ii)

The water inside the sealed container evaporated, forming water vapour. The water vapour came into contact with the cooler walls of the container (or the cooler surrounding air outside the container). The water vapour lost heat and condensed into water droplets on the surface of the container.

Kenny set up an experiment as shown in the diagram below. He dropped a metal ball from a height of 1 m above the ground. The soft toy moved upwards when the metal ball hit the ramp.

He repeated the experiment by releasing the metal ball at different heights and recorded the height at which the soft toy reached in the table as shown below.

B (m)S (m)
10.6

(a)

What should Kenny do to ensure that the results he obtained are reliable?

Kenny heated the metal ball for 10 minutes. He repeated the experiment by releasing the metal ball at the height of 1 m.

(b)

Circle the correct answer.
The height at which the soft toy reached will be ( lower than / the same as / greater than ) the height the soft toy reached before the metal ball was heated.

(c)

Explain your answer in part (b).

Answer

(a)

Kenny should repeat the experiment multiple times (at least 3 times) for each height and calculate the average height the soft toy reached, to ensure the results are reliable and consistent.

(b)

The height at which the soft toy reached will be the same as the height the soft toy reached before the metal ball was heated.

(c)

Since the mass of the metal ball remains the same after heating, and it is released from the same height of 1 m, the gravitational potential energy of the ball is unchanged. This results in the same amount of kinetic energy being transferred to the ramp when the ball hits it, so the soft toy will reach the same height as before.

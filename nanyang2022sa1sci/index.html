  1. A and C only
  2. A and D only
  3. B and C only
  4. B and D only

Answer

Option 3 (B and C only). B is true — the Sun is the ultimate source of energy for all living things. C is true — during photosynthesis light energy from the Sun is transferred to (stored in) the food the plant makes.

Question 19

2 marks

Which of the following does not involve energy conversion?

  1. Typing on the computer.
  2. Keeping a lit candle burning.
  3. Capturing light in solar panels.
  4. Leaving a cup of hot water to cool.

Answer

Option 4. When a cup of hot water is left to cool, heat energy simply transfers from the hot water to the cooler surroundings. The energy stays as heat throughout — it is not changed (converted) into a different form.

The others all convert energy: typing converts kinetic/electrical energy to electrical and other forms; a burning candle converts chemical (stored) energy to light and heat; solar panels convert light energy to electrical energy.

Question 27

2 marks

Equal volumes of hot water at 80°C were poured into four identical containers of different materials. Which of the following containers will most likely contain water at the lowest temperature after two hours?

The four options are: (1) plastic container, (2) wooden container, (3) glass container, (4) metal container.

  1. plastic container
  2. wooden container
  3. glass container
  4. metal container

Answer

Option 4 (metal container). Metal is the best conductor of heat among the four materials. It lets heat from the hot water escape to the surroundings fastest, so after two hours the water in the metal container loses the most heat and is at the lowest temperature.

Plastic, wood and glass are poorer conductors (better insulators), so the water in them stays warmer for longer.

2 marks

Two similar-sized pots, R and S, were placed on similar-sized stoves as shown below. 800 ml of water was poured into pot R while 600 ml of water was poured into pot S. The stoves were turned on at the same time.

The water in both pots took the same amount of time to reach boiling point. Which of the following could have resulted in this observation?

A   Stove R gave out more heat than stove S.
B   Pot R was a better conductor of heat than pot S.
C   The water in pot R had more heat than the water in pot S at the start.

  2. C and D only

Question 39

5 marks

(a) What is a force? [1]

Mabel set up an experiment. She glued the spring to the table as shown below. She then attached a copper block on the spring. The length of the compressed spring was 10 cm.

(b) State the force(s) acting on the copper block. [1]

Mabel then removed the 500 g copper block and attached a 500 g iron block.

(c) What would Mabel observe about the length of the spring? Explain her observation. [2]

Finally, she replaced the iron block with a 500 g magnet. She observed that the length of the spring was less than 10 cm.

(d) Explain her observation. [1]

Answer

(a) A force is a push or a pull.
(b) Gravitational force (the weight of the block pulling down) and elastic spring force (the spring pushing up).
(c) The spring would increase in length (compress less / stretch more towards the magnet). Iron is a magnetic material, so the iron block is attracted upwards to the strong magnet above it. This upward pull reduces the push of the block on the spring, so the spring is compressed less and becomes longer.
(d) The like poles of the two magnets were facing each other, so the magnets repelled. This pushed the block down harder onto the spring, compressing it further so its length became less than 10 cm.

Question 40

4 marks

Lauren bought a hot drink which was served in a cup as shown below.

Lauren held the cup and almost scalded her fingers. The stall holder offered her a cup sleeve to put around her cup as shown below. She noticed that the inner side of the cup sleeve was corrugated.

(a) Explain how the corrugated inner side of the cup sleeve will prevent Lauren from scalding her fingers. [2]

When Lauren got home, she poured a cold drink into a double-walled cup to keep it cold for a long time. The double-walled cup has a layer of air between the two walls of the cup as shown below.

(b) Explain how the double-walled cup helps to keep her cold drink cold for as long as possible. [2]

Answer

(a) The corrugated (bumpy) inner side reduces the surface area of the hot cup that is actually in contact with the sleeve, and it traps air spaces between the cup and the sleeve. Air is a poor conductor of heat, so heat is conducted from the hot cup to Lauren's fingers more slowly — her fingers gain less heat and she is not scalded.
(b) The layer of air between the two walls is a poor conductor of heat. So heat from the warmer surroundings is conducted to the cold drink more slowly, keeping the drink cold for a longer time.

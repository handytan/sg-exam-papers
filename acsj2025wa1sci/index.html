Question 1

2 marks

A solar panel is used to produce electricity for a lamp that is used at night.

Which form of energy received by the solar panel is converted to electricity?

  1. light energy
  2. sound energy
  3. kinetic energy
  4. potential energy

Answer

Option 1. A solar panel converts light energy from the Sun into electrical energy. Sound, kinetic and potential energy are not the input energy the panel receives, so options 2, 3 and 4 are wrong.

Question 3

2 marks

The diagram shows a man wearing a head torch, powered by batteries, around his head to help him see in the dark during outdoor activities.

Which of the following correctly shows the energy changes in the head torch when it is turned on?

  1. electrical energy → light energy + heat energy
  2. potential energy → heat energy → light energy
  3. potential energy → electrical energy → light energy + heat energy
  4. electrical energy → potential energy → light energy + heat energy

Answer

Option 3. The batteries store chemical (potential) energy, which is converted to electrical energy, then to light energy (and some heat energy) at the bulb. Option 1 omits the stored chemical energy in the batteries; options 2 and 4 put the conversions in the wrong order.

Question 6

2 marks

Which two are examples of the effects of forces?

A   The umbrella blocking the sunlight.
B   A boy bouncing a ball against a wall.
C   Mother sweeping the floor with a broom.
D   A metal spoon gaining heat when placed in a bowl of hot soup.

  1. A and B
  2. A and D
  3. B and C
  4. C and D

Answer

Option 3. A force can change the motion, direction or shape of an object. B (a bouncing ball changes direction) and C (a force moves the broom to sweep) are effects of a force. A is the blocking of light, and D is heat transfer — neither involves a force acting on an object, so they are excluded.

Question 8

2 marks

Ramps are often used when transporting goods on a trolley to a container truck. The surface of the ramp is often textured like the one shown in the image.

Which statement explains the reason for the textured surface of the ramp?

  1. It strengthens the ramp and prevents breakage.
  2. It reduces wear and tear to the wheels so that the trolley can last longer.
  3. It increases friction between the goods and the trolley to prevent the goods from dropping.
  4. It increases friction between the wheels and the surface of the ramp to prevent the trolley from slipping.

Answer

Option 4. A rough, textured surface increases friction between the wheels and the ramp, giving the trolley better grip so it does not slip. The texture is not about strength (1), and it acts between the wheels and the ramp, not the goods and trolley, so options 2 and 3 are wrong.

Question 10

2 marks

Jin went for an indoor skydiving activity. Air was continuously blown from the bottom of the flying chamber to keep him afloat at position X.

Which of the following explains why Jin was able to stay afloat at position X?

  1. Gravitational potential energy of Jin is converted to kinetic energy.
  2. Kinetic energy of the moving air is transferred to Jin, keeping him afloat.
  3. The upward force exerted by the moving air on Jin is greater than his weight.
  4. Gravitational force acting on Jin was equal to the upward force exerted by the moving air on him.

Answer

Option 4. Jin stays floating at a fixed position X, so he is balanced — the upward force of the moving air equals his weight (gravitational force). If the upward force were greater (option 3) he would rise rather than stay still. Options 1 and 2 describe energy transfers, not why the forces are balanced.

Question 12

3 marks

Jim uses a falling cylinder to split wooden logs. The 5 kg cylinder is lifted to a height, and when released, it slides down the rod and strikes the blade, which then splits the log.

(a) Where does Jim’s energy come from? [1]
(b) Jim lifts the cylinder and releases it. As it falls, not all its potential energy is converted to kinetic energy. Explain why. [1]

Answer

(a) From the food that Jim eats (chemical potential energy stored in food).
(b) Some of the potential energy is converted to heat energy (due to friction between the cylinder and the rod), so not all of it becomes kinetic energy.
(c) (i) Lift the cylinder to a greater height before releasing it (use a longer rod and drop from the highest point). (ii) Use a heavier cylinder (increase the mass).

Question 16

4 marks

Elliott tied object X to the top of his plastic toy car and placed it on a track that only allowed the car to travel in a straight line. At the end of the track, he attached a strong magnet to the wall as shown.

Elliott pushed the car with object X towards the end of the track and gently released it at distance P. The car moved backwards and travelled a distance Q before stopping. He repeated the process with decreasing distances P and measured the new distance Q each time.

(a) Name the force that caused the car to move backwards. [1]
(b) Explain why the car moved backwards with object X. [2]

Elliott repeated the experiment using the same setup, but this time he increased distance P. He observed that at a certain distance P, the car with object X did not move at all.

(c) Explain Elliott’s observation. [1]

Answer

(a) Magnetic force.
(b) Object X is a magnet, and the like poles of object X and the magnet on the wall face each other. The two like poles repel each other, and this magnetic force of repulsion pushes the car (with object X) away from the wall, causing it to move backwards.
(c) When distance P is large enough, object X is too far from the wall magnet. The magnetic field is too weak at that distance, so the magnetic force of repulsion acting on the car is too small to move it, and the car does not move.

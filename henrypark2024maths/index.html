In the figure, XQY and PQR are straight lines. $\angle XQZ = 90°$, $\angle ZQW = 130°$ and $\angle RQY = 35°$. Find $\angle PQW$.

  2. $95°$
  3. $105°$

[2]

$\dfrac{1}{6}$ of the books in a class library were fiction books and the rest were non-fiction books. When the number of fiction books was increased by 100% and the number of non-fiction books increased by 50%, Mr Lim found that he had an additional 168 books in the library. How many books were there in the class library at first?

  1. 112
  2. 144
  3. 288
  4. 294

Worked Solution

Let total books at first $= T$.

Fiction $= \dfrac{1}{6}T$, Non-fiction $= \dfrac{5}{6}T$

Additional fiction (100% increase) $= \dfrac{1}{6}T$

Additional non-fiction (50% increase) $= \dfrac{1}{2} \times \dfrac{5}{6}T = \dfrac{5}{12}T$

Total additional $= \dfrac{1}{6}T + \dfrac{5}{12}T = \dfrac{2}{12}T + \dfrac{5}{12}T = \dfrac{7}{12}T = 168$

$T = 168 \times \dfrac{12}{7} = 288$

Answer: (3) 288

In the figure below, PQUW is a square and QRSV is a rhombus. QW is parallel to RT and $\angle QRS = 70°$. PQR and WUS are straight lines. Find $\angle VRT$.

Worked Solution

In rhombus QRSV, adjacent angles are supplementary:

PQUW is a square, so $\angle PQU = 90°$. PQR is a straight line, so $\angle UQR = 90°$.

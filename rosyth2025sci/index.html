Question 28

2 marks

When the circuit is closed, the bar magnet moved from position A to B as shown in the diagram below.

Which of the following shows the conversion of energy that explains the movement of the bar magnet from position A to B when the circuit is closed?

  • potential energy → kinetic energy → electrical energy
  • potential energy → electrical energy → kinetic energy
  • kinetic energy → electrical energy → potential energy
  • potential energy → electrical energy → kinetic energy → potential energy

Answer

Option 4. Potential energy → electrical energy → kinetic energy → potential energy. The batteries store chemical (potential) energy. When the circuit is closed, this potential energy is converted to electrical energy flowing through the circuit. Shahim made a lantern with three light bulbs. The bulbs are controlled with a switch.

Shahim constructed a new circuit using three new bulbs, X, Y and Z, and a switch:

  • If any one bulb fuses, the other two will still light up.
  • Three bulbs will light up only when the switch is closed.

(b) The diagram below shows part of the new circuit. Complete the circuit so that it will work as described above. [2]

(c) Based on the circuit arrangement in (b), what would happen to the brightness of bulbs X and Y if bulb Z fuses? [1]

Answer

(a)

The three bulbs were arranged in a series circuit. In a series circuit, all components share the same path for current flow. When one bulb fuses, it creates a break (open circuit) in the only path for current. Since the circuit is broken, no current can flow, and the other two bulbs cannot light up.

(b)

The three bulbs X, Y, and Z must be connected in parallel with each other, and the switch is connected in series with the parallel combination (so the switch controls all three bulbs together). In a parallel circuit, each bulb has its own independent path. If one fuses, current can still flow through the other two paths, keeping them lit. The switch in series ensures all three only light up when the switch is closed.

(c)

The brightness of bulbs X and Y would increase. In a parallel circuit, when bulb Z fuses (creates an open circuit in Z's branch), the total resistance of the circuit decreases slightly, and more current flows through the remaining branches (X and Y). With more current flowing through X and Y, they become brighter. (Note: in an ideal parallel circuit with a battery, the voltage across X and Y stays the same, but the total current from the battery changes.)

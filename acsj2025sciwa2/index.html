Question 2

2 marks

Which of the following is not a function of the skeletal system?

  1. To support the body
  2. To protect internal organs
  3. To give the body its shape
  4. To break down food into simpler substances

Answer

Option 4. Supporting the body, protecting internal organs and giving the body its shape are all jobs of the skeletal system. Breaking down food into simpler substances is digestion, which is carried out by the digestive system, not the skeleton.

Question 5

2 marks

The diagram shows a food web.

Which of the following organisms are both prey and predator?

  1. C and D
  2. B, C and G
  3. D, E and F
  4. E, F and G

Answer

Option 3. An organism that is both prey and predator must eat another organism and also be eaten by another organism. D, E and F each feed on something and are themselves eaten by something further along the web, so they are both predator and prey. B is only prey (it is eaten but eats nothing in the web) and G is only a predator (it eats but is not eaten), so they are excluded.

Question 10

2 marks

The diagram shows the pathway of a baseball after it was thrown into the air.

Which of the following statement(s) is/are true?

W: There are no forces acting on the ball at point D.
X: The baseball has maximum gravitational force at B.
Y: A push force was exerted on the baseball at point A.
Z: Heat is produced as the ball rolls along from point C to point D.

  1. W only
  2. W and X only
  3. Y and Z only
  4. X, Y and Z only

Answer

Option 3. Y is true — the thrower exerts a push force on the ball at A. Z is true — friction between the rolling ball and the ground from C to D produces heat. W is false because gravity (and friction) still act on the ball at D, and X is false because gravitational force does not become maximum at the top of the flight (B). So only Y and Z are correct.

Question 12

4 marks

Devi built a musical toy as shown. She used two identical batteries, a bulb, a switch and a sound box, S. When the switch was closed, the bulb lit up and the toy played music. When the bulb fused, the toy could still play music.

(a) Use a pencil to draw the wires in the circuit that Devi has set up in her toy. [2]

(b) State one way that Devi can increase the brightness of the bulb in her toy. [1]

(c) Mark on the circuit with an X where Devi should add another switch so that she can use the toy only as a night light. [1]

Answer

(a) The sound box S and the bulb are wired in two parallel branches across the two batteries (joined in series), with the switch in the main circuit. Because the bulb is on its own branch, the toy can still play music through the sound box even after the bulb fuses:
(b) Add another battery to the circuit (so that more electric current flows through the bulb).
(c) The extra switch should be placed on the bulb’s branch only (marked X above). Opening it switches off only the bulb (the night light) while the music can still play, and closing it lets the toy work as a night light. Placing the switch in series with the bulb alone lets her control the light independently.

Question 13

4 marks

Mrs Lum placed a packet of fish balls at room temperature into a pot of boiling soup as shown.

(a) The temperature of the soup decreased after the fish balls were added. Give a reason why. [1]

(b) It takes 15 minutes to cook a packet of fish balls. How will the time taken to cook the fish balls change if Mrs Lum cooks two packets of fish balls in the same pot using the same volume of hot soup instead? Explain why. [1]

Mrs Lum steamed some buns with chocolate filling as shown in diagram 1.

(c) After steaming the buns for 10 minutes, the chocolate filling burst through the walls of the buns as shown in diagram 2. Explain why. [2]

Answer

(a) The fish balls were at a lower temperature than the boiling soup, so heat was transferred from the hotter soup to the cooler fish balls. As the soup lost heat to the fish balls, its temperature decreased.
(b) The time taken to cook would increase. With two packets there is more fish ball (a greater mass) to heat up using the same volume of soup, so it takes longer for all the fish balls to gain enough heat to be cooked.
(c) As the buns were steamed, the chocolate filling gained heat and expanded. The chocolate filling expanded more than the walls of the bun could stretch, so it burst through the walls of the buns.

Question 16

5 marks

(a) What is a community? [1]

There are four populations, Q, R, S and T, in community U. The information about these populations is as follows:

  • R feeds on Q.
  • T feeds on Q and R.
  • S is eaten by Q and T.

(b) In the space below, draw a food web to represent the relationship among the four populations. [2]

(c) Which organism is a plant-and-animal-eater? Explain your answer. [1]

(d) A disease caused a sharp decrease in the population of organism R. Explain how this will affect the population of organism S. [1]

Answer

(a) A community is made up of all the different populations of organisms living together in the same habitat.
(b) Each arrow points from the organism being eaten to the organism that eats it. S is eaten by Q and T; Q is eaten by R and T; R is eaten by T:
(c) Organism T is the plant-and-animal-eater. S is a producer (a plant, as it is eaten but does not eat anything). T feeds on Q and R, and Q feeds on the plant S, so T eats both plant material (through Q) and animals (Q and R). Therefore T eats both plants and animals.
(d) When R decreases sharply, fewer R are left to feed on Q, so the population of Q increases. With more Q, more S is eaten by Q, so the population of organism S will decrease.

Cacti are plants that can be found in the desert, where temperatures can be extremely high during the day and very low at night. Water is scarce, and the environment is dry and harsh.

Cacti have needle-like leaves which help them survive for long periods without rain.

(a) Give a reason how having spines instead of leaves helps the cacti survive in the desert. [1]

(b) State another structural adaptation of the cacti that helps them survive long periods without rain. [1]

Bird C is an organism that lives in the cacti and feeds on the nectar of the cacti flower as shown in diagrams 1 and 2.

Diagram 1
Diagram 2

(c) How do Bird C and the cacti benefit from one another? [2]

Answer

(a) The needle-like spines have a much smaller surface area than broad leaves, so the cactus loses much less water through evaporation. This reduces water loss and helps the cactus survive the dry desert with little rain.
(b) The cactus has a thick, fleshy, waxy stem that stores a large amount of water inside it. This stored water allows the cactus to survive long periods without rain. (The waxy surface also reduces water loss.)

Ali wanted to find out if the presence of a wing-like structure affects the distance travelled by fruit X.

He conducted an experiment with the following materials: a fan, two fruit X with wing-like structure, and a measuring tape.

(a) List down the steps Ali took to conduct his experiment to investigate his aim. [2]

(b) State two variables to keep constant to ensure a fair test. [1]

(c) State an advantage to the young plant when the fruit travels a further distance from the parent plant. [1]

Answer

(a) 1. Remove the wing-like structure from one of the two fruit X, leaving the other fruit X with its wing-like structure.
2. Turn on the fan and drop both fruits from the same height in front of the fan.
3. Use the measuring tape to measure and record the distance travelled by each fruit, then compare the two distances.
(b) Any two of: the mass/size of the two fruit X; the height from which the fruits are dropped; the speed/setting of the fan; the distance of the fruits from the fan.
(c) When the fruit travels further from the parent plant, the young plant that grows from it does not have to compete with the parent plant for resources such as space, water, sunlight and mineral salts.

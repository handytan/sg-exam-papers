Question 36

5 marks

The diagram below shows the water cycle.

(a) State a difference between evaporation and boiling. [1]

Metal sails are used to build a structure to obtain freshwater in open spaces in the desert. In the desert, it is hot in the day and cold at night.

(b) Based on the conditions in the desert and how the set-up works, explain whether more freshwater will be collected in the day or at night. [2]
(c) State one change that can be made to the structure to collect more freshwater within the same amount of time. Explain how the change helps to collect more freshwater. [2]

Answer

(a) Evaporation takes place at any temperature (at the surface of a liquid), while boiling only occurs at a specific fixed temperature (the boiling point) throughout the liquid.
(b) More freshwater will be collected at night. At night, the desert is cold — the metal sails lose heat quickly (metal is a good conductor of heat) and become colder than the surrounding air. The warm, moist air in the atmosphere comes into contact with the cold metal sails, causing water vapour in the air to condense into water droplets on the metal surface. These water droplets run down and are collected as freshwater. During the day, it is hot, so the metal sails are warm and condensation does not occur — instead evaporation takes place.
(c) Use larger or more metal sails to increase the total surface area. A larger surface area allows more water vapour from the air to come into contact with the cold metal surface and condense into water droplets. This means more water droplets are formed and more freshwater is collected in the same amount of time.

The diagrams below show the position of lamps in three rooms of a dollhouse and its circuit diagram. When the switch is closed, all the bulbs light up.

(a) Siti removed one of the bulbs from the circuit and the other two bulbs did not light up. Which bulb did Siti remove? Explain your answer. [1]
(b) Siti wanted to rearrange the bulbs, A, B and C, such that when one of the bulbs fused, the two remaining bulbs will continue to light up. Complete the circuit diagram to show this new arrangement. [1]
(c) State a disadvantage of the new arrangement of bulbs in (b). [1]

Answer

(a) Siti removed Bulb C. In the circuit, bulbs are in series. When Bulb C is removed, it creates an open (broken) circuit, so no current can flow through the circuit and the remaining bulbs A and B cannot light up. (Bulb C is positioned such that removing it breaks the only path for current flow.)
(b) The new arrangement should connect bulbs A, B, and C in parallel with each other. In a parallel circuit, each bulb has its own separate path for current. If one bulb fuses (breaks), current can still flow through the other paths, so the remaining two bulbs continue to light up.
(c) A disadvantage of the parallel arrangement is that the batteries/cells will run out (go flat) more quickly because more current is drawn from the batteries (each bulb draws its own current), so the batteries need to be replaced more frequently.

The diagram below shows a conveyor belt in a factory. A light sensor is used to count the number of objects moving on the conveyor belt.

The result from the light sensor and datalogger is shown in the graph below.

(a) From the graph, how many objects passed the sensor? Explain your answer. [2]
(b) Another object P made of frosted glass passed through the light sensor. Draw on the graph below to show what the reading from the light sensor would look like when object P passes through it. [1]
(c) Give a reason why the amount of light shown on the graph never drops to zero. [1]

Answer

(a) 5 objects passed the sensor. When an object passes the sensor, it blocks the light from the light source, causing the amount of light detected by the sensor to decrease (drop to a lower level). Each dip in the graph represents one object passing the sensor. By counting the number of times the light level decreased/dipped, we can count 5 objects passing the sensor.
(b) For object P made of frosted glass: frosted glass is translucent — it allows some light to pass through but scatters it. The graph should show a partial dip (the light level drops but does NOT drop as low as when an opaque object passes through — it stays at an intermediate level, higher than the dips caused by the opaque objects). The dip is shallower than the previous dips in the graph.
(c) The amount of light never drops to zero because the surroundings of the light sensor are not completely dark. Some light from the environment (room lighting, background light) can still reach and be detected by the sensor even when an object is blocking the main light source.

Question 40

3 marks

Eva designed a set-up to automatically turn on a warning light when too much rainwater is collected in a roadside drain during heavy rain. This happens when the water pipe used to remove rainwater is clogged. Her set-up is shown below.

(a) Describe and explain how the warning light is turned on automatically when the water pipe is clogged. [2]
(b) Without adding new parts to the set-up, suggest one modification to the set-up so that the warning light turns on earlier when it rains less heavily. [1]

Answer

(a) When the water pipe is clogged, rainwater cannot drain away and begins to accumulate in the drain. As the water level rises, the wooden block (which floats on water) floats upward. The metal sheet attached to the wooden block also rises. When the water level is high enough, the rising metal sheet touches the metal rods, completing the electrical circuit. With the circuit complete, electric current flows through the circuit and switches on the warning light automatically.
(b) Lower the position of the metal rods (push the metal rods deeper into the drain / closer to the normal water level). By positioning the metal rods lower, the metal sheet (attached to the floating wooden block) needs to rise only a small amount before it touches the metal rods and completes the circuit. This means the warning light will be triggered at a lower water level, turning on earlier even when it is raining less heavily.

Question 1

2 marks

Which of the following statements are true?

A   All living things depend directly on plants for food.
B   The Sun is the main source of energy for all living things.
C   Energy is transferred from the Sun to plants during photosynthesis.
D   Only water, oxygen and sunlight are needed for a plant to make food.

  • A and C only
  • A and D only
  • B and C only
  • B and D only

Answer

Option 3 (B and C only). Statement B is true — the Sun is the main source of energy for all living things. Statement C is true — during photosynthesis, light energy from the Sun is captured by the plant and converted (transferred) into the plant's food.
Statement A is false: not all living things depend directly on plants; many animals are meat-eaters that depend on plants only indirectly. Statement D is false: a plant needs water, carbon dioxide and sunlight (and chlorophyll) to make food — it uses carbon dioxide, not oxygen.

Question 7

Which of the above organism(s) is/are decomposer(s)?

  • fungi only
  • fungi and fern only
  • ant and earthworm only
  • earthworm and fern only

Answer

Option 1 (fungi only). Decomposers are organisms that break down dead plants and animals (and waste) into simpler substances. Of the four organisms, only fungi are decomposers. Question 28

2 marks

When railway tracks are built, gaps are left between them to allow for expansion of the tracks so that they would not be damaged. Each section of the railway track, part X, is of the same length.

Which one of the following information would be most useful to a person building the railway tracks?

  • Shortest length of part X on the coldest day
  • Greatest length of part X on the hottest day
  • Greatest length of part X on the coldest day

Answer

Mabel set up an experiment. She glued the spring to the table as shown below. She then attached a steel block on the spring. The length of the compressed spring was 10 cm. A strong magnet is fixed above the block.

(d) Suggest what object X could be. [1]

Answer

(a) A force is a push or a pull.
(b) The forces acting on the steel block are: gravitational force (gravity), the elastic spring force from the compressed spring pushing up, and the magnetic force of attraction pulling it up towards the strong magnet.
(c) The length of the spring would decrease (become shorter than 10 cm). Wood is a non-magnetic material, so it is not attracted upwards by the strong magnet. This happens if X is a magnet placed with a like pole facing the fixed magnet above, so that the two magnets repel and the magnet above pushes object X down, compressing the spring further.

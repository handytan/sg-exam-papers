Question 3

2 marks

After the battery of the phone is fully charged, it is unplugged from the socket and then switched on.

Which of the following represents the energy conversion of the phone when it is switched on?

  1. heat energy → electrical energy → sound and light energy
  2. kinetic energy → electrical energy → sound and light energy
  3. electrical energy → potential energy → sound and light energy
  4. potential energy → electrical energy → sound and light energy

Answer

Option 4. A fully charged battery stores chemical (potential) energy. When the phone is switched on, this stored potential energy is converted to electrical energy, which the phone then converts to sound (speaker) and light (screen) energy. The phone is unplugged, so it does not start from heat, kinetic or electrical energy from the mains.

Question 5

2 marks

Which of the following is not an example of energy conversion?

  1. light passing through a glass
  2. walking up an overhead bridge
  3. lighting a candle with a matchstick
  4. generating electricity in a power station

Answer

Option 1. Energy conversion means one form of energy changing into another. When light simply passes through a glass, the light energy stays as light energy — no conversion occurs. In the others energy is converted: walking up a bridge converts chemical (in the body) to potential and kinetic energy; lighting a candle converts chemical energy to heat and light; a power station converts other forms (e.g. kinetic/chemical) into electrical energy.

Question 8

2 marks

Patrick released a ball from Point W as shown. The ball rolled down the slope, moved along the carpet flooring and stopped at point Z.

Which statements are correct?

A  At points X and Y, the ball had kinetic energy only.
B  The ball would have rolled down faster if slope was shorter.
C  The ball had the greatest amount of kinetic energy at point W.
D  The ball would have rolled further if the ball was released at a higher height than Point W.

  1. A and C only
  2. B and D only
  3. A, B and D only
  4. B, C and D only

Answer

Option 2. Statement B is correct: a shorter, therefore steeper, slope makes the ball speed up more quickly. Statement D is correct: releasing the ball from a greater height gives it more potential energy, which converts to more kinetic energy, so it rolls further. Statement A is wrong — at X and Y (still on the slope) the ball still has some potential energy as well as kinetic energy. Statement C is wrong — at W the ball is released from rest, so it has no kinetic energy there (greatest potential energy, greatest kinetic energy is at the bottom of the slope).

Question 9

3 marks

Pei Hwa carried out an experiment in a dark room. He placed a plant in a closed clear glass container as shown.

(a) Give a reason why a clear glass container was used for the experiment. [1]

(b) Pei Hwa switched off the lamp. State what would happen to the amount of oxygen and the amount of carbon dioxide detected by the datalogger after a few hours. Explain. [2]

Answer

(a) A clear glass container allows light to pass through (into the container) so that the plant is able to photosynthesise. [1]
(b) The amount of oxygen detected will decrease and the amount of carbon dioxide detected will increase. [1] With the lamp switched off there is no light, so the plant cannot photosynthesise (and so does not take in carbon dioxide or give out oxygen). However the plant continues to respire, taking in oxygen and giving out carbon dioxide. [1]

Question 10

4 marks

In an experiment, an inflated balloon was glued to the straw. At X, when the air was released from the balloon, it moved forward. At Y, all the air had escaped from the balloon but it continued to move forward. The balloon came to a stop at Z.

(a) Give a reason why the balloon continued to move forward from Y towards Z. [1]

(b) Explain why the balloon finally stopped at Z. [1]

(c) Using the same set-up, suggest a way to make the balloon move further than Z. Explain your answer in terms of energy conversion. [2]

Answer

(a) At Y the balloon still has some kinetic energy, so it continues to move forward. [1]
(b) By Z, all the kinetic energy of the balloon has been converted into heat and sound energy (due to friction and air resistance). With no more kinetic energy, the balloon stops. [1]
(c) Blow more air into the balloon (inflate it more) before releasing it. [1] The balloon then has more (elastic) potential energy, which is converted into more kinetic energy, so the balloon moves further than Z. [1]

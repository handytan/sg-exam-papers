[2 marks]Q30. Charlie had equal numbers of \$2 and \$10 notes. After spending \$20 (from \$10 notes) and exchanging remaining \$10 notes for \$5 notes, he had equal \$2 and \$5 notes. How much money did he have at first?
Answer: \$48
Spent \$20 → used 2 × \$10 notes → had n−2 \$10 notes remaining.
Exchange (n−2) \$10 notes → 2(n−2) \$5 notes = n \$2 notes → 2n−4 = n → n = 4
Total = 4 × \$2 + 4 × \$10 = \$8 + \$40 = \$48

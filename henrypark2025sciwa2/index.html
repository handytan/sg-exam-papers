Question 5

The diagram shows the human digestive system.

Which of the following is correct?

  1. Digestion is completed in part N.
  2. Parts J, K and M do not produce digestive juices.
  3. Part M absorbs digested food into the bloodstream.
  4. Parts L and N break down food into simpler substances.

Answer

Option 3. Part M is the small intestine, where digested food is absorbed into the bloodstream. Question 6

2 marks

Which one of the following correctly compares the process of fertilisation in both flowering plants and humans?

  1. Both involve the production of eggs.
  2. Both involve the fusion of male and female reproductive cells.
  3. Both involve the transfer of eggs from the male to the female.
  4. Both involve the development of foetus in the ovary of the female.

Answer

Option 2. Fertilisation in both flowering plants and humans is the fusion of a male reproductive cell with a female reproductive cell. Eggs are produced only by the female (not by the male), eggs are never transferred from male to female, and a foetus develops only in humans (in the uterus, not the ovary), so the other options are wrong.

Question 13

2 marks

Jackie removed the outer ring of the stem from a plant between P and Q as shown. Only the food-carrying tubes were cut away with this outer ring.

After ten days, which of the following are likely observations that Jackie could make about the plant?

A  Part Q will be swollen.
B  Part P will be swollen.
C  The leaves have dried up.
D  The fruits have grown bigger.

  1. A and B only
  2. B and D only
  3. B, C and D only
  4. A, B, C and D

Answer

Option 2. Removing the ring cuts only the food-carrying tubes (phloem). Food made in the leaves moves down but cannot pass the cut, so it accumulates above the cut at P, making part P swell (B), and the extra food helps the fruits above the cut grow bigger (D). The water-carrying tubes are intact, so the leaves stay alive (C wrong) and part Q below the cut does not swell (A wrong).

Question 16

4 marks

Annie set up an experiment in a dark room to find out which coloured light(s) — red, blue or green — could be used for photosynthesis. She divided a fish tank into 3 sections, P, Q and R, using black plastic sheets as shown. The coloured lights are of the same brightness. Before adding the water plants, the fish could be found in all three sections. She then placed a water plant into each section. After some time, the fish gathered in sections P and R only.

(a) Based on Annie's experiment, which coloured light(s) could be used for photosynthesis? [1]

(b) Explain your answer in part (a). [2]

(c) Annie removed all the water plants after a day. What would she most likely observe about the fishes in the set-up after two days? Explain your answer. [1]

Answer

(a) Red light and blue light.
(b) The fish gathered in sections P and R, where red and blue light were used. In these sections the water plants could photosynthesise and give out oxygen, which the fish need for respiration, so the fish moved to where there was more oxygen. In section Q (green light) the plant could not photosynthesise well, so little oxygen was given out.
(c) The fish would swim up to the water surface to breathe. With the plants removed, no oxygen is being added to the water, so the fish go to the surface to obtain oxygen from the air.

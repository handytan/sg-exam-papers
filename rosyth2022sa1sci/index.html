Question 1

2 marks

Which parts of the human digestive system produce digestive juices?

  1. stomach and small intestine only
  2. mouth, gullet and large intestine only
  3. mouth, stomach and small intestine only
  4. mouth, gullet, stomach and small intestine only

Answer

Option 3. Digestive juices are produced in the mouth (saliva), the stomach (gastric juice) and the small intestine. The gullet and large intestine do not produce digestive juices — the gullet only pushes food down and the large intestine mainly absorbs water — so options that include them are wrong.

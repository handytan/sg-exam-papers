Jazzy set up the experiment as shown below.

Section A: __________

Section B: __________

(d)

Explain why more water is collected if the heater is placed in the water. [2m]

Answer

Section A: Evaporation (liquid water gains heat and changes into water vapour/gas).

Section B: Condensation (water vapour loses heat and changes back into liquid water droplets).

The heater provides heat energy to the water in Section A so that the water can evaporate faster/more quickly, producing more water vapour that then travels to Section B to condense.

Metal is a better conductor of heat than plastic. If the heater is placed in the water (instead of above/outside the water), the water is heated more directly and gains heat faster. This causes the water to evaporate faster, producing more water vapour. More water vapour travels to Section B where it condenses, resulting in more water being collected in Section B.

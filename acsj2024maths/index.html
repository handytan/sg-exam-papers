Paper 1 · Booklet A — Question 14

[2]

At first, Mrs Ang had twice as many red beads as yellow beads. She used $\frac{2}{3}$ of her yellow beads and some of her red beads to make necklaces. In the end, $\frac{3}{5}$ of the beads left were red. What fraction of her red beads did Mrs Ang use?

  1. $\frac{1}{2}$
  2. $\frac{2}{5}$
  3. $\frac{3}{4}$
  4. $\frac{7}{12}$

Worked Solution

Let yellow $= 3u$, red $= 6u$. After using $\frac{2}{3}$ of yellow: yellow left $= u$.
Let red used $= r$. $\frac{3}{5}(u + 6u - r) = 6u - r \Rightarrow 3(7u-r) = 5(6u-r) \Rightarrow 2r = 9u \Rightarrow r = \frac{9u}{2}$.
Fraction of red used $= \frac{9u/2}{6u} = \frac{3}{4}$.

Answer: (3) $\frac{3}{4}$

(a) What was the volume of water in Tank A at first? [1]

(b) The base area of the small container is 60 cm² and the base area of each large container is 120 cm². Find the capacity of the small container. [3]

Worked Solution

(a)

Tank A dimensions (from figure): $30 \times 10 \times 15$ cm. Volume $= \frac{2}{5} \times 30 \times 10 \times 15 = 1800\ \mathrm{cm}^3$.

(a) 1800 cm³

(b)

Small base $= 60\ \mathrm{cm}^2$; each large base $= 120\ \mathrm{cm}^2 = 2 \times$ small. All same height $h$.
Total volume: $60h + 2 \times 120h = 300h = 1800 \Rightarrow h = 6$ cm.
Capacity of small $= 60 \times 6 = 360\ \mathrm{cm}^3$.

(b) 360 cm³

Paper 2 — Question 14

[5]

Peter designed a logo for a poster as shown. (Take $\pi = \dfrac{22}{7}$)

(a) What was the total area of the shaded logo? [2]

(b) What was the perimeter of the logo? [3]

Worked Solution

(a)

Area of large quadrant (radius 14): $\frac{3}{4} \times \frac{22}{7} \times 14^2 = 462\ \mathrm{cm}^2$.
Area of semi-circles (radius 3.5): $\frac{3}{2} \times \frac{22}{7} \times 3.5^2 = 57.75\ \mathrm{cm}^2$.
Total shaded area $= 462 + 57.75 = 519.75\ \mathrm{cm}^2$.

(a) 519.75 cm²

(b)

Perimeter of one section: quarter-circle arc $= \frac{1}{4} \times 2 \times \frac{22}{7} \times 14 = 22$ cm; full circle circumference (r=7) $= \frac{22}{7} \times 14 = 44$ cm. One X shape perimeter $= 22 + 44 = 66$ cm. Adding two straight sections: $66 + 18 = 84$ cm.
Total perimeter (3 identical sections) $= 84 \times 3 = 252$ cm.

(b) 252 cm

Paper 2 — Question 15

[3]

Tina had a total of 754 pearl necklaces and bead necklaces for sale. After selling twice as many pearl necklaces as bead necklaces, she had $\frac{1}{3}$ of the pearl necklaces and $\frac{1}{4}$ of the bead necklaces left. What was the total number of pearl and bead necklaces left?

Worked Solution

Let bead sold $= x$, pearl sold $= 2x$.
Left pearl $= \frac{1}{3}$ of pearl $\Rightarrow$ sold $= \frac{2}{3}$ of pearl $= 2x \Rightarrow$ pearl $= 3x$.
Left bead $= \frac{1}{4}$ of bead $\Rightarrow$ sold $= \frac{3}{4}$ of bead $= x \Rightarrow$ bead $= \frac{4x}{3}$.
Total: $3x + \frac{4x}{3} = \frac{13x}{3} = 754 \Rightarrow x = \frac{754 \times 3}{13} = 174$. So $x = 174$... Let me use units:
$\frac{1}{3}$ pearl $= \frac{3}{4}$ bead (both equal sold bead $x$). Let pearl $= 9u$, bead $= 4u$. Total $= 13u = 754 \Rightarrow u = 58$.
Left $= \frac{1}{3}(9u) + \frac{1}{4}(4u) = 3u + u = 4u = 4 \times 58 = 232$.

Answer: 232

Paper 2 — Question 16

[4]

At a school bookshop, erasers are only sold in packs of 4 and pencils are only sold in packs of 5.

Mr Lim spent \$139.45 buying erasers and pencils. He put all the erasers and pencils into bags. The ratio of erasers to pencils in each bag was 2 : 3. How many pencils did he buy?

Worked Solution

Eraser : pencil $= 2:3$. Scale to whole packs: 20 erasers (5 packs of 4) and 30 pencils (6 packs of 5) — this is one “set”.
Cost per set: $5 \times \$1.99 + 6 \times \$2.99 = \$9.95 + \$17.94 = \$27.89$.
Number of sets: $\$139.45 \div \$27.89 = 5$ sets.
Total packs of pencils $= 6 \times 5 = 30$ packs. Pencils $= 30 \times 5 = 150$.

Answer: 150 pencils

Paper 2 — Question 17

[5]

Gary spent $\frac{5}{8}$ of his money on 10 identical notebooks and 10 identical files. Then, he spent $\frac{5}{6}$ of his remaining money on a bag.

(a) What fraction of Gary's money was spent on the bag? [1]

(b) Each file cost \$4 more than each notebook and the bag cost \$38 more than each notebook. How much money had Gary left? [4]

Worked Solution

(a)

Remaining after notebooks and files $= \frac{3}{8}$. Bag $= \frac{5}{6} \times \frac{3}{8} = \frac{5}{16}$.

(a) $\dfrac{5}{16}$

(b)

Let notebook $= \$n$. File $= \$(n+4)$, bag $= \$(n+38)$.
10 notebooks + 10 files $= 10n + 10(n+4) = 20n + 40 = \frac{5}{8} \times$ total.
Bag $= n + 38 = \frac{5}{16} \times$ total. So bag $\times 2 = \frac{5}{8} \times$ total $= 20n + 40$.
$2(n + 38) = 20n + 40 \Rightarrow 2n + 76 = 20n + 40 \Rightarrow 18n = 36 \Rightarrow n = \$2$.
Total $= (20 \times 2 + 40) \div \frac{5}{8} = 80 \times \frac{8}{5} = \$128$.
Spent: $80 + 40 = \$120$. Left $= \$128 - \$120 = \$8$.

(b) \$8

Question 19

2 marks

Which one of the following is not an example of energy conversion?

  • cooling hot water in a cup
  • toasting bread in a toaster
  • climbing down a flight of stairs
  • generating electricity in a power station

Answer

Option 1. Cooling hot water is not an energy conversion — it is a transfer of heat energy from the water to the surroundings. The form of energy (heat) remains the same, it just moves. By contrast: toasting bread converts electrical energy to heat and light energy; climbing down stairs converts gravitational potential energy to kinetic energy; generating electricity converts kinetic energy (from steam turbines) to electrical energy. All of options 2, 3, and 4 involve a change in the form of energy.

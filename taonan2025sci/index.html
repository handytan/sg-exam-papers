Lisa prepared two set-ups using two cups, A and B, of the same size and material. Cup A is black and Cup B is white. She wants to find out how colours affect the amount of heat absorbed.

Both set-ups were placed under the Sun for two hours. Lisa recorded the change in temperature of the water in each cup in the graph below.

(a) State one other variable that Lisa must keep constant to ensure a fair test. [1]
(b) Based on the results, what can Lisa conclude about the experiment? [1]

Animal X has two types of fur, Type 1 and Type 2. It sheds its fur and grows the other type of fur during hotter and colder months of the year.

Characteristic of furType 1Type 2
Thickness of furThinThick
Colour of furLightDark
(c) Based on Lisa's findings, would animal X grow Type 2 fur during the hotter or colder months of the year? Explain your answer. [1]
(d) Explain how the thickness of animal X's fur further helps it to survive during the colder months of the year. [1]

Answer

(a) One variable Lisa must keep constant: the volume of water in each cup, OR the material/thickness of the lids, OR the thickness of each cup (to ensure a fair test with only colour as the variable).
(b) Lisa can conclude that black absorbs more heat than white. The black cup (A) showed a greater temperature increase, meaning it absorbed more heat from the Sun than the white cup (B).
(c) Animal X would grow Type 2 fur (dark, thick) during the colder months. Dark fur absorbs more heat from the surroundings, helping to keep the animal warm during colder months.
(d) During the colder months, the thick fur traps air between the fur fibres. Air is a poor conductor of heat, so it reduces heat loss from the animal's body to the cold surroundings, helping the animal stay warm.

Question 39

3 marks

An elastic rope was used to make a ride at the amusement park.

At the start of every ride, the seat that is attached to the elastic rope is pulled to position H to allow the rider to get on. When released, the stretched elastic rope pulled the rider until he reached the maximum height at point Q. After which he is pulled down. He moved up and down continuously before gradually coming to a stop.

(a) Identify two forces that made the rider move up and down continuously. [1]

The graph below shows how the length of another two elastic ropes, S and T, increased when weights were hung on them.

To make the ride less scary, the maximum height is decreased to point P as shown in Diagram 2.

(b) Which elastic rope, S or T, should be used so that the maximum height of the ride will not exceed point P? Explain your answer. [2]

Answer

(a) The two forces that made the rider move up and down continuously are: Elastic (spring) force and Gravitational force.
(b) Rope S should be used. From the graph, rope S stretches longer than rope T when the same weight is hung on it — S is less stiff (more elastic). When the rider is placed at position H (same starting position), rope S, being less stiff, exerts a smaller elastic spring force on the rider. This smaller upward force means the rider will not be launched as high, so the maximum height will not exceed point P, making the ride less scary.

Question 40

4 marks

Ada has a stuffed toy with a built-in voice recorder.

The circuit diagram of the voice recorder is shown below.

When Ada hugs the stuffed toy, a voice recording is played.

(a) State the property of the metal contacts that allow the voice recording to be played. [1]
(b) Explain why the voice recording is heard when Ada hugs the toy. [1]

Ada played with the stuffed toy for a month.

(c) State a possible change in the voice recording played. Explain your answer. [1]
(d) The eyes of the toy also light up when she hugged the toy. Complete the circuit diagram below such that the bulbs will light up the brightest possible. The bulbs have been drawn for you. [1]

Answer

(a) The metal contacts are conductors of electricity (they allow electric current to flow through them).
(b) When Ada hugs the stuffed toy, the metal contacts A and B are pressed together and touch each other. This closes/completes the circuit, allowing electric current to flow through the voice recorder, and the voice recording is played.
(c) The voice recording will be softer/quieter. After a month of use, the battery becomes weaker (less electrical energy stored), so less current flows through the circuit, and the voice recorder produces a softer sound.
(d) The two bulbs should be connected in parallel with each other (and with the battery). In a parallel circuit, each bulb receives the full voltage from the battery, making them shine at their brightest possible. (If connected in series, they would share the voltage and be dimmer.)

Question 41

3 marks

Mr Lim exerted a force on a cupboard to move it from point A to point B as shown in the diagram below.

(a) State two forces acting on Mr Lim when he was pushing the cupboard. [1]
(b) Besides pushing harder, what could Mr Lim do to the cupboard to decrease the time taken to move it? Explain your answer. [2]

Answer

(a) Two forces acting on Mr Lim: (1) Gravitational force (pulling him downward) and (2) Frictional force (acting on his feet/shoes against the floor as he pushes).
(b) Mr Lim could apply a lubricant (e.g., oil or grease) to the base of the cupboard. The lubricant reduces the friction between the cupboard and the floor, so less force is needed to move it and it can be moved more quickly.
OR
Mr Lim could place the cupboard on a trolley with wheels. The wheels reduce friction between the cupboard and the floor, allowing the cupboard to move more easily and quickly.
OR
Mr Lim could remove all items from inside the cupboard. With less weight, the frictional force opposing the cupboard's movement is reduced, making it easier and faster to push.

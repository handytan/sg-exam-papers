Option 1. Condensation is water vapour turning into water droplets to form clouds, which happens at B (rising vapour cooling into cloud). Question 2

2 marks

Set-ups J and K were placed on a table. They contained equal amounts of water at 70 °C and equal number of ice cubes as shown. The beaker in set-up K was narrower.

After a while, more water droplets formed in set-up J. Which statement explains this observation?

  1. More water evaporated because the water was warmer.
  2. More water condensed because the ice cubes were colder.
  3. More water evaporated because the exposed surface area of water was larger.
  4. More water vapour condensed because there was less air movement in the beaker.

Answer

Option 3. Beaker J is wider, so the water has a larger exposed surface area and evaporates faster, producing more water vapour. This extra vapour then condenses on the cold surface, forming more water droplets in J. The water temperature and number of ice cubes are the same in both set-ups, so options 1 and 2 are wrong.

Question 4

2 marks

Which of the following is not an effect of a force?

  1. a pump inflating a balloon
  2. a mirror reflecting an image
  3. a boy kicking a soccer ball coming towards him
  4. a piece of paper being folded into an aeroplane

Answer

Option 2. A force can change an object’s shape, speed or direction of movement. Inflating, kicking and folding all change shape or motion, so they are effects of a force. A mirror reflecting an image is a property of light, not the result of a force acting on an object.

Which force(s) acted on the sliding block?

Elastic spring forceGravitational forceFrictional force
(1)yesyesyes

Answer

Option 4. Once the block has left the spring and is sliding freely, the spring is no longer touching it, so there is no elastic spring force. Gravitational force still pulls the block down, and frictional force from the tabletop acts against its motion (this is what eventually stops it). So only gravitational and frictional forces act.

Question 6

2 marks

In the set-up, the bulb will light up if the load has a mass of 15 kg or more.

How can the set-up be changed so that the bulb will light up if the load has a mass of 12 kg?

  1. increase the length of the contacts
  2. use longer springs of the same type
  3. use stronger springs of the same length
  4. increase the thickness of platform but of the same mass

Answer

Option 1. The bulb lights when the platform sinks far enough for the contacts to touch and complete the circuit. Making the contacts longer means a smaller compression (a lighter load) is enough to close the gap, so a 12 kg load will work. Stronger or longer springs would need a heavier load, and the platform change does not affect the gap.

Question 8

2 marks

Hock Siang placed a ping pong ball on a mouthpiece as shown. He then blew into the mouthpiece and observed the ball hovering in mid-air for a short period of time.

Which statement(s) explained why the ball was able to hover in mid-air?

A — The force exerted by the moving air was greater than the weight of the ball.
B — The warm air exhaled from the mouth heated the air up and caused the ball to rise.
C — The gravitational force acting on the ball was greater than the force exerted by the moving air.

  1. A only
  2. A and B only
  3. B and C only
  4. A, B and C

Answer

Option 1. The ball rises and hovers because the upward force from the moving (blown) air is greater than the weight of the ball, so only statement A is correct. Question 10

2 marks

A tree supports only the organisms shown.

Which of the following is correct?

  1. The squirrels form a community.
  2. The tree is a habitat for the organisms.
  3. The birds and the tree form one community.
  4. There are four populations of organisms in the habitat.

Answer

Option 2. A habitat is the place where organisms live, so the tree is correctly the habitat for these organisms. The squirrels alone are a population, not a community (a community needs all the different populations together), and a community is made of living things only, so the tree cannot be part of one.

Question 11

3 marks
(a) State what boiling means. [1]

Melvin wanted to cook some eggs at 80 °C. When the water boiled, he removed the pot from the stove and placed it on a metal tabletop. He added some cold water before putting in some eggs. He covered the pot for 12 minutes.

(b) Without reheating the pot, suggest two ways that Melvin can do to ensure that the water was kept at about 80 °C in the same pot. [2]
(i) To reduce heat loss from the water:
(ii) To supply heat to the water:

Answer

(a) Boiling is the change in state from a liquid to a gas that takes place rapidly throughout the liquid at a fixed temperature (the boiling point).
(b)(i) To reduce heat loss: Cover the outside of the pot (and the lid) with a material that is a poor conductor of heat, or stand the pot on a poor conductor such as a wooden tabletop, so that less heat is lost from the water to the cooler surroundings.
(b)(ii) To supply heat: Add more water that is already at 80 °C (or some boiling water) to the pot. The added hot water transfers heat to the cooling water, keeping its temperature at about 80 °C.

Question 12

4 marks

Keith placed a wooden block on a ramp as shown.

When angle X was 5°, the block did not slide down the ramp. He gradually increased angle X by making the ramp steeper until the block first began to slide, and recorded angle X. He repeated this using ramps made of two other surfaces. The results are shown.

Type of surfaceAngle X (°)
rubber45
wood30
plastic20
(a) When angle X was 5°, the block did not slide down the ramp. Explain why. [1]
(b) Explain why angle X was smaller for the plastic surface as compared to the wooden surface. [1]

The picture shows a staircase with anti-slip tapes pasted to the steps to help prevent slipping while walking down.

(c) Based on the results, which material is most suitable for making the anti-slip tape? Explain why. [2]

Answer

(a) At 5° the frictional force between the wooden block and the surface of the ramp was greater than the gravitational force pulling the block down the slope, so there was no resultant force to make the block slide.
(b) The plastic surface was smoother than the wooden surface, so there was less frictional force between the block and the plastic. Less friction means the ramp only needed to be tilted to a smaller angle X before the block began to slide.
(c) Rubber is the most suitable. From the results, angle X was the largest (45°) for rubber, showing that rubber produces the greatest frictional force before sliding. This greater friction between the anti-slip tape and the soles of people’s shoes best helps to prevent slipping.

Question 13

3 marks

Aloysius conducted an experiment using the set-ups as shown. He added the same amount of water onto four similar cloths, W, X, Y and Z.

The graph shows the mass of each cloth over a period of time.

(a) Based on the graph, explain how the different distances of the lamp from the cloth affect the mass of the cloth. [2]
(b) Based on the graph, which variable, light or wind, has a smaller effect on the mass of the cloth? Give a reason. [1]

Answer

(a) The further the lamp is from the cloth, the lower the temperature of the surroundings around the cloth. This causes the rate of evaporation of the water to decrease, so less water leaves the cloth and the mass of the cloth stays greater (as seen for cloth X compared with cloth W).
(b) Wind has the smaller effect. Compare set-ups at the same distance so that only light versus wind differs. At 30 cm, cloth W (light) loses much more mass than cloth Y (wind); at 70 cm, cloth X (light) loses more mass than cloth Z (wind). In both pairs the wind set-up lost less mass, so wind has the smaller effect on the mass of the cloth.
